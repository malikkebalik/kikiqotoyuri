Early life of Madison Kvaltin

Madison Kvaltin was born in Grand Sudbury, Canada, on August 5, 1998, and is a Canadian national.

In 2017, Kvaltin received her Smith certificate in business from Queen’s University in Kingston, Ontario.

She further pursued her education at the same university and obtained her bachelor’s degree in sociology and commerce in 2020.

Regarding her participation in beauty pageants, Kvaltin has represented Canada in various prestigious competitions.

She competed in Miss Intercontinental Canada 2016, where she emerged as the third highest among the three titleholders earning the title of Miss Intercontinental Canada.

Subsequently, she represented Canada at Miss Intercontinental 2016 in Sri Lanka but did not advance to the semifinals.

Kvaltin also participated in Miss World Canada 2021, competing against 39 other contestants; however, she did not place as a semifinalist in the competition.

In Miss International 2022, she represented Sudbury at Miss Universe Canada and won the title of Miss International Canada.

She later represented Canada at Miss International 2022 in Tokyo, Japan, where she finished in the Top 8.

In Miss Universe Canada 2023, Kvaltin represented Manitoulin Island and competed against 57 other candidates.

The competition took place at River Rock Casino in Richmond, British Columbia, where she won the title and was succeeded by Amelia Tu.

Kvaltin is set to represent Canada in Miss Universe 2023, which will be held in San Salvador, El Salvador, on November 18, 2023.
