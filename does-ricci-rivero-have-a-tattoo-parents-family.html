Does Ricci Rivero have a Tattoo? Many fans of De La Salle Green Archers are searching for his tattoo. 

Ricci Rivero is a Filipino basketball player.

He played college basketball for the De La Salle University Green Archers in the University Athletic Association of the Philippines (UAAP) from 2016 to 2018.

After that, he changed teams and played for the University of the Philippines Fighting Maroons from 2019 to 2021.

On the basketball court, Rivero is renowned for his athleticism, quickness, and scoring prowess.

In several international basketball competitions, such as the FIBA 3×3 Asia Cup and the FIBA World Cup qualifications, he has represented the Philippines.

Similarly, Rivero signed a contract with the Taoyuan Pilots of the P. League+ on June 24, 2022. Nevertheless, he was released before the season started.

Does Ricci Rivero Have A Tattoo?

Yes, Ricci Rivero has a Tattoo on his left hand.

Ricci’s supporters discovered he had a tattoo when a Twitter user asked him if the marking on his hand was a tattoo.

He hasn’t revealed anything else about his tattoo other than this. Also, the design and significance of his tattoo still need to be discovered.

Ricci Rivero Parents And Family

Ricci Rivero comes from a family of basketball players.

In the 1980s, his Father, Paolo Rivero, participated in UAAP basketball with the De La Salle Green Archers.

From 2014 to 2018, his older brother Prince Rivero participated in the UAAP competition for the De La Salle Green Archers.

His Father has shown support for Ricci’s basketball career by showing up to his events and games.

But Ricci hasn’t revealed much about his mother in interviews with or on social media. Yet he has acknowledged and appreciated both of his parents and frequently posts pictures of them on social media.

Similarly, Ricci has two other siblings: a younger brother named Rasheed Rivero and a younger sister named Raine Rivero.

Rivero has mentioned on social media that he enjoys spending time with his family and frequently posts images with them. However, it is still being determined if they are also interested in basketball.

Rivero traveled to Manila to finish his secondary education at La Salle Green Hills in Mandaluyong after being born and raised in Ilagan, Isabela Province.
